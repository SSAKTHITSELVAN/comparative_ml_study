# A Comparative Study of Machine Learning Models for Short-Term Stock Price Direction Prediction Using Public Market Data

## Journal Information

### Aims & Scope

IEEE Access is a multidisciplinary, open access, applications-oriented, all-electronic archival journal that continuously presents the results of original research or development across all of IEEE's fields of interest. IEEE Access publishes articles that are of high interest to readers, original, technically correct, and clearly presented. Supported by author publication charges (APC), its style is more practical and less theoretical than most IEEE Transactions, providing a complement to traditional IEEE periodicals.

**Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:**

- Artificial intelligence and machine learning
- Data science, big data analytics, and data mining
- Signal processing and pattern recognition
- Computer science and information technology
- Computational intelligence and soft computing
- Financial engineering and computational finance
- Applied statistics and predictive modeling
- Emerging technologies in engineering and science

IEEE Access is especially interested in interdisciplinary and cross-domain applications of these technologies, including applications in financial markets, healthcare, energy systems, and other real-world domains.

### Editorial Board

IEEE Access operates under a large, multidisciplinary editorial board overseen by the Editor-in-Chief. The journal's editorial structure includes:

- **Editor-in-Chief:** Prof. Derek Abbott, University of Adelaide, Australia
- **Associate Editors:** Over 2,000 associate editors from institutions worldwide, covering all IEEE technical fields

The editorial board members are selected based on their expertise across IEEE's diverse fields of interest. Each submission is handled by an associate editor with domain expertise relevant to the manuscript's topic. For manuscripts in the areas of machine learning, data science, and computational finance, associate editors with backgrounds in:

- Machine learning and artificial intelligence
- Statistical learning and pattern recognition
- Financial technology and quantitative analysis
- Signal processing and time series analysis

are typically assigned to manage the peer review process.

**Review Process:**
- Single-blind peer review
- Target turnaround: 4-6 weeks from submission to first decision
- Binary accept/reject decision (no revise-and-resubmit)
- Minimum of 3 independent reviewers per manuscript

### Instructions for Authors

**Manuscript Preparation:**

1. **Format:** Manuscripts must be prepared using the IEEE Access LaTeX or Microsoft Word templates. The IEEEtran LaTeX class (journal mode) is recommended.

2. **Length:** There is no strict page limit. However, manuscripts should be as concise as possible while maintaining completeness. Typical research articles range from 8-20 pages.

3. **Structure:** Manuscripts should include:
   - Title, author names, and affiliations
   - Abstract (maximum 250 words)
   - Index terms (keywords)
   - Introduction with clear statement of contributions
   - Related work / literature review
   - Methodology
   - Experimental results and discussion
   - Conclusion
   - References

4. **Figures and Tables:** All figures must be of high quality (minimum 300 dpi). Tables should use the booktabs style. All figures and tables must be referenced in the text.

5. **References:** Use the IEEE citation style (numbered references in square brackets). A minimum of 20-30 references is expected for a full research article.

6. **Data Availability:** Authors are encouraged to make datasets and code publicly available to support reproducibility.

**Submission Process:**

- Submit via IEEE's ScholarOne Manuscripts portal
- Include a cover letter describing the novelty and significance of the work
- Suggest at least 5 potential reviewers with full contact information
- Declare any conflicts of interest

**Article Processing Charge (APC):**

- Standard APC: $1,950 USD per accepted article
- IEEE members receive a discount
- Fee waivers available for authors from low-income countries

**Ethical Requirements:**

- All research must comply with ethical standards
- Plagiarism check via iThenticate (similarity index should be below 20%)
- Authors must declare that the work is original and not under consideration elsewhere
- Any use of AI writing tools must be disclosed
